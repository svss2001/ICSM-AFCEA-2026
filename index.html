ICSM-AFCEA-2026

The International Conference on “Synergy in Mathematics: Advances in Fractional Calculus and Engineering Applications” (ICSM-AFCEA 2026) will provide an international platform for the exchange of ideas, presentation of original research findings, and fostering of collaborations among participants. The conference will feature keynote lectures by eminent experts, invited talks, oral and poster presentations, and interactive discussions on various aspects of mathematics, engineering, and their applications. ICSM-AFCEA 2026 will provide a forum for researchers to share their latest work in Fractional Calculus, Applied Mathematics, and Engineering Applications through presentation and discussion in scientific sessions.

Fractional calculus has emerged as a powerful mathematical framework for modeling memory, hereditary, and nonlocal effects in complex systems that are often beyond the reach of classical integer-order models. Its growing relevance in dynamical systems, control theory, signal processing, bio-mathematics, material science, fluid mechanics, thermal sciences, data-driven modeling, and emerging interdisciplinary engineering problems has opened new directions for both theoretical research and practical innovation. Contemporary conferences and special sessions in this area consistently emphasize its role in modeling complex real-world systems and in connecting rigorous mathematical theory with applications across science and engineering.

The conference aims to bring together experts working on fractional differential equations, special functions, integral transforms, stability analysis, numerical and computational techniques, optimization, mathematical modeling, and engineering systems to exchange ideas, present original research, and foster new collaborations. In particular, the event seeks to highlight the synergy between foundational mathematics and application-oriented engineering research, encouraging dialogue between theoretical mathematicians and researchers applying advanced analytical tools to practical problems in technology, physical sciences, biological systems, and industrial processes. Similar fractional-calculus-focused conferences describe the field as a bridge between mathematical theory and applications in physics, engineering, biology, and computation, with strong emphasis on modeling, simulation, and interdisciplinary problem-solving.

Tracks

A central objective of the conference is to provide a premier academic forum for presenting recent advancements and exploring interdisciplinary frontiers in mathematical analysis, fractional calculus, and their engineering applications. Key topics include:

Fractional Calculus and Fractional Differential Equations
Special Functions, Integral Transforms, and Operator Theory
Numerical and Computational Methods in Fractional Systems
Dynamical Systems, Control, and Optimization
Mathematical Modeling in Physical and Engineering Sciences
Fractional Calculus in Biological, Medical, and Environmental Systems
Data-Driven Mathematics, AI, and Emerging Interdisciplinary Applications
Pure and Applied Mathematical Analysis
Recent Advances in Engineering Applications of Mathematics
Interdisciplinary Frontiers in Fractional Calculus

Note: The conference scope is not limited to the above topics. Submissions are welcome on any topic related to applied mathematics and computing, across all domains.
